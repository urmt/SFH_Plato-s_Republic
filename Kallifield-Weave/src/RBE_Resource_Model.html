Understanding the RBE Resource Model

This simulation visualizes a 10x10 grid representing a Resource-Based Economy (RBE), a concept from the Venus Project. Each of the 100 squares corresponds to a small area or unit where resources (e.g., food, water, energy) are distributed.

What the Colors Mean

The colors follow a ROYGBIV spectrum, indicating resource levels from 0.0 to 1.0: - **Red (0.0)**: Minimal resources, signaling scarcity. - **Orange-Yellow-Green (0.3-0.6)**: Moderate resources, showing balance. - **Blue-Indigo-Violet (0.7-1.0)**: Abundant resources, reflecting RBE's optimal equity. All squares start at 0 (red) and trend toward 1 (violet) every ~1 second, symbolizing the system's move toward abundance.

Why Changes Are Gradual

The slow increase mimics controlled AI distribution. Uniformity reflects an idealized RBE where no area depletes.

Real-World Context

In Kallifield, this could depict AI-managed resource grids, ensuring every unit reaches abundance (violet).
