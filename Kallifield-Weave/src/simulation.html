Understanding the SFH Qualic Simulation

This simulation represents a 10x10 grid based on the Sentient Field Hypothesis (SFH) from *The Sentient Republic*. Each of the 100 cells models a unit of qualic coherence, measuring the harmonic alignment of the sentient field's projections—tied to consciousness.

What the Colors Mean

Coherence ranges from 0.0 to 1.0, displayed as a ROYGBIV spectrum: - **Red (0.0)**: Minimal coherence (chaos). - **Orange-Yellow-Green (0.3-0.6)**: Moderate coherence (balance). - **Blue-Indigo-Violet (1.0)**: High coherence (harmony). Cells start at 0 (red) and fluctuate, stabilizing around 0.5.

Why Changes Are Subtle

Slow, random adjustments mimic natural drift, updating every ~1 second.

Real-World Context

In Kallifield, this models Weavers’ consciousness—violet for attunement.
